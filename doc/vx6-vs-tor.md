# VX6 vs Tor

Suggested title: VX6 vs Tor | A Faster Fit for Direct Services, Internal Tools, and Peer Applications

Suggested meta description: VX6 is not a Tor clone. It is an IPv6-first peer-to-peer network built for direct services, localhost sharing, private infrastructure, and team workflows.

## Important Positioning

Do not say:

Tor has been replaced.

Say this instead:

VX6 is a better fit for direct services, localhost sharing, private tools, and team infrastructure.

## The Simple Difference

Tor is known for anonymous browsing and onion routing.  
VX6 is built for peer-to-peer service access, direct infrastructure workflows, relay paths, and localhost-to-localhost sharing.

## Where VX6 Feels Stronger

### Real Services

VX6 is made for:

- SSH
- web apps
- admin panels
- APIs
- databases
- internal tools

### Direct Paths

If direct IPv6 is possible, VX6 can use it.  
That can make access cleaner and faster than sending everything through older-style proxy assumptions.

### Localhost Model

VX6 works especially well when the app is already on localhost.  
That is a powerful everyday advantage for operators and teams.

### Team Infrastructure

VX6 fits internal architecture and peer applications naturally.  
It is closer to how engineers actually deploy and use services.

## Safer Comparison Copy

- Built for workloads Tor was never designed for.
- Faster for direct services and private team networks.
- Better for apps, tools, and localhost-to-localhost workflows.
- A service network, not just a browsing path.

## Short Comparison Table

Tor:

- browser-first reputation
- anonymity-centered model
- not naturally shaped around direct localhost service sharing

VX6:

- service-first model
- IPv6-first peer network
- direct, named, proxied, and hidden access modes
- strong fit for infrastructure and collaboration workflows
